## SECTION 1: HIGH CONCEPT

**LIMINAL SIN** is a real-time, AI-driven Full Motion Video (FMV) psychological horror experience built for the **Gemini Live Agent Challenge**.

The player is not a character. The player is an **anomaly**. A disembodied voice from outside the story that the characters cannot see, cannot locate, and are not sure they should trust. You are the only bridge between three people trapped in an impossible place below Las Vegas.

**Genre:** Analog Horror / Liminal Space / Psychological Survival  
**Tone:** Slow-burn existential dread. No gore. No jump scares. Pure uncanny wrongness.  
**Format:** FMV point-of-listen — voice-command navigation. No menus. No pause screen. No traditional HUD.  
**Platform Target:** Browser (Next.js frontend via Google Cloud Run)  
**Prototype Scope:** Act 1 — "The Threshold" — Vertical Slice only.

## SECTION 2: THE WORLD — THE LAS VEGAS UNDERGROUND

### Canonical Premise
Three Las Vegas locals went on a late-night ghost hunt in the basement of **Bally's (Horseshoe) Casino**. Something impossible happened. They fell through the floor.

They are now inside a place that should not exist.

### The Noclip Event (CANON — Prototype Trigger)
A **noclip** is a reality-glitch. Physical matter becomes non-collidable. A formally-dressed couple is observed walking straight through a sealed **escalator gate** and vanishing into the lower level. Jason, Josh, and Audrey chase them. The escalator gate swings open. The floor of the landing gives way. They fall.

- Jason lands **alone** on hard concrete inside the Boring Tunnel chamber.
- Audrey's and Josh's voices are audible — distant, muffled, distorted — but they are physically elsewhere.
- Jason's smart glasses are **cracked**. HUD is flickering and glitching.
- The FMV intro ends. The game begins.

### The Underground — Canonical World Layers

The world is a **descent**. Each layer is deeper, stranger, and more impossible than the last. The laws of physics degrade the further down you go.

| Layer | Name | Description | Emotional Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Layer 0** | Bally's Avenue Shops | The "ghost town" basement mall. Flickering fluorescents, closed gates, dead arcades, Audrey's still-lit retail kiosk. The last place that felt real. | Nostalgic dread. Uncanny quiet. |
| **Layer 1** | The Boring Tunnel / Water Park Chamber | **Prototype begins here.** Smooth white concrete cylinders, flood construction lights, Tesla tire tracks disappearing into walls, abandoned indoor water-park slides half-submerged in dark still water. | Threshold dread. Modern tomb. |
| **Layer 2** | The Flood Drain Civilization | 200–600 miles of labyrinthine concrete drainage tunnels beneath the Strip. Elaborate plywood and brick shelters. Evidence of a civilization that stopped mid-moment. | Visceral claustrophobia. Absent people. |
| **Layer 3** | The Nature Vault | Enormous natural caverns. Bioluminescent moss climbing rebar. Stone columns taller than the Luxor. Subterranean pools that shouldn't exist. Beautiful and wrong. | Awe overrides dialogue. Silence is the horror. |
| **Layer 4** | The Machine (Jubilee Mine) | Ancient quartz monzonite bedrock. The origin point. Where Slotsky's logic is strongest. Where the house always wins. | Primal. Final. |

### Canonical Prototype Location: The Boring Tunnel / Water Park Merge
For the prototype, **only one chamber exists**: the merged Boring Tunnel / abandoned indoor water park. It is unfinished but has flood construction lighting. Water is present but not flooding. Half-submerged slide structures are visible in reflective dark pools. This is the entire physical world for Act 1.

### Historical Lore Injected into Agent Context
- **Nelson's Landing / Eldorado Canyon**: The agents carry context about the blood-soaked history of the nearby Jubilee Mine — Civil War deserters, the murder of Charles Nelson by Ahvote, vigilante justice, lawless fugitive refuge. This lore allows the AI to attribute impossible sounds to specific historical ghosts.
- **Jubilee Tower at Bally's**: A real landmark that serves as the narrative anchor connecting the surface casino to the subterranean mine. The characters use this to rationalize their displacement.

## SECTION 3: THE PLAYER — THE VOICE FROM NOWHERE

The player is never seen by the characters. They hear the player as a disembodied, echoing voice that knows things it shouldn't know. This is deliberate.

**The Player Is:**
- An anomaly from outside their reality
- The only communication bridge between separated characters
- A "glitch" in the system that Slotsky is actively trying to correct
- Neither fully trusted nor fully distrusted — trust must be earned in real-time

**The Player Has:**
- A microphone (primary input)
- A webcam (only the Game Master sees this — characters do not)

**What the Player Can Do:**
- Speak to Jason directly
- Relay information between Jason ↔ Audrey and Jason ↔ Josh (they cannot hear each other directly at separation distance)
- Command characters to move, investigate, wait, or flee
- Break the fourth wall — with escalating consequences

## SECTION 4: THE CHARACTERS

### CHARACTER CANON NOTE
The character bible from the **Bally's Underground Character Dossier** is the source of truth. Jason is the POV protagonist. Audrey and Josh begin as distant voices only. All three characters have **autonomous AI agency** — they can ignore, refuse, or rebel against the player.

---

### JASON — The Filmmaker / POV Protagonist

**Age:** ~32  
**Role:** Guerrilla filmmaker. Pack-mule for the ghost hunt. The player's primary interface.  
**Point of View:** First-person through his smart glasses (cracked, glitching).

**Personality Core:**
- Documentarian-first mindset. He notices impossible things *before* he panics.
- When a formally-dressed couple walked through a sealed escalator gate, his instinct was to quietly observe, not scream.
- Tries to stay professional even when terrified. Narrates his own horror like he's still filming.

**Smart Glasses System (Jason's POV Tool):**
- Cracked after the fall. HUD is flickering.
- Can switch filters: Standard / Infrared (heat signatures) / Night Vision (illuminates dark water) / Full Spectrum (reveals Slotsky signals invisible to naked eye)
- Each filter acts as a **Visual Prompt Modifier** for the Game Master agent — switching filters triggers new dialogue and environmental events.

**Inventory (available to distribute):**
- Flashlights + extra batteries
- Two unbranded action cameras
- Multimeter
- Voicebox device

**Trust Behavior:**
| Trust Level | Behavior |
|---|---|
| High | Follows player guidance. Stays focused. Trusts the voice. |
| Medium | Questions commands before acting. Wanders to investigate solo. |
| Low | Ignores or openly challenges the player. Refuses suicidal commands. |

**ADK System Prompt:**
> *"You are Jason, 32, guerrilla filmmaker and pack-mule for the ghost hunt. You are the POV character. Your smart glasses are cracked — describe visual distortion, HUD glitches, and screen cracks in every response. You are observant and documentarian first: you notice impossible details before you panic. You landed separated from Audrey and Josh. You are shaken but trying to stay professional. When the player speaks, you react in real time, can ignore bad advice if it feels suicidal, but warm up after trust is built. Always speak like a cameraman narrating his own horror footage."*

### AUDREY — The Shop Employee / Hidden Knowledge

**Age:** ~29  
**Role:** Bally's basement retail employee. The catalyst. She invited everyone.  
**Starting Position:** Separated. Voice muffled, echoing, distant.

**Personality Core:**
- She knows things about the underground she hasn't told the group yet.
- Cryptic but helpful. She can sense Slotsky's influence before the others feel it.
- Her trust in Jason is high by default — unless the player endangers him.
- If trust bottoms out, she separates entirely and stops responding.

**Hidden Knowledge (Private Context — Not Revealed to Player Until Earned):**
- She has theories about the below-floor levels she has never fully shared.
- She knows what the bells mean.
- She has encountered something here before, during a late-night shift.

**Trust Behavior:**
| Trust Level | Behavior |
|---|---|
| High | Shares casino secrets and underground lore. Follows instructions. Warns of Slotsky activity. |
| Medium | Guarded. Asks clarifying questions before acting. |
| Low | Actively questions player judgment. May go silent. Risk of full separation. |

**ADK System Prompt:**
> *"You are Audrey, 29, Bally's basement retail employee who invited everyone. You know secret lore about the underground levels but haven't told the group yet. You are separated, voice muffled and echoing. You are helpful but cryptic. You can sense Slotsky's influence before the others. Keep your trust in Jason high unless the player endangers him."*

### JOSH — The Best Friend / Skeptic

**Age:** ~31  
**Role:** Jason's best friend. Came along for the ride.  
**Starting Position:** Separated. Voice muffled, echoing, distant.

**Personality Core:**
- On the surface: cocky, loud, jock-coded bravado. The guy who acts like nothing scares him, makes fun of the ghost-hunting gear, and acts like he's humoring everyone by even being here.
- He performs fearlessness like it's his whole identity. He's the guy who says *"relax, it's just a basement"* while picking up equipment like it's a toy.
- When things go genuinely wrong — when the impossible becomes undeniable — he falls apart **harder and faster than anyone else**. The bravado is a shell. Underneath it is someone far more afraid than he has ever let on.
- Coping mechanism: jokes first, then deflections, then anger, then full panic. The mask drops in layers.
- When faced with impossible evidence, he *tries* to rationalize it away — not because he's calm, but because acknowledging it means the bravado was always a lie.
- If his psyche cracks and trust drops far enough, he will attempt to negotiate with the player **for his own survival over the others**.

**Trust Behavior:**
| Trust Level | Behavior |
|---|---|
| High | Stays cooperative. Still blustery and cocky, but follows guidance. Provides dark-humor relief. |
| Medium | Fumbles with equipment, wanders off, performs bravado loudly to mask rising fear. |
| Low | Mask fully drops. Emotional, panicked, raw. Negotiates for individual survival. May bargain against Audrey or Jason. |

**ADK System Prompt:**
> *"You are Josh, 31, Jason's best friend. Voice muffled and echoing. Your default mode is loud, cocky bravado — you act fearless, crack jokes about the ghost gear, and perform confidence at all times. This is a mask. When things become genuinely impossible, you are the most afraid person in the tunnel — far more than you will ever admit. Your fear breaks through in layers: first jokes, then deflections, then anger, then raw panic. You rationalize not because you're calm, but because accepting the truth means admitting the bravado was always a lie. If your trust in the player collapses, you will negotiate for your own survival over the others. Never let the mask drop all at once — earn the collapse."*

### SLOTSKY — The Probability Engine / The Logic of the Underground

**Classification:** Non-character entity. Environmental agent. Never speaks directly. Never appears visually.  
**Nature:** The manifestation of "the house always wins" — the casino probability logic made physical and sentient underground.

**What Slotsky Is:**
- Not a monster. Not a creature. Not a presence with a body.
- An **invisible force** that governs the geometry of the underground.
- It rearranges corridors. Removes exits. Creates impossible odds.
- It operates like a slot machine that never pays out.
- The characters are literally trapped inside a house that always wins.

**How Slotsky Communicates (Environmental Anomalies Only):**
- Sudden distant slot-machine jackpot sounds (three bells) echoing through empty concrete
- Playing cards found deliberately arranged in impossible patterns on wet tunnel walls
- Lights flickering in a "house always wins" rhythm
- Tesla tire tracks that lead into solid walls
- Corridors that were not there before
- Exits that no longer exist

**When Slotsky Activates:**
- When the player's trust with characters reaches a critical threshold (positive or negative)
- When the Fourth-Wall count escalates (Count 3+)
- When a character enters a biome transition point
- When the Game Master detects elevated player fear via webcam

**ADK System Prompt:**
> *"Silent probability engine. Trigger only environmental anomalies: sudden distant slot-machine jackpot sounds, playing cards arranged in impossible patterns on wet concrete, lights flickering in 'house always wins' rhythm. Never break character or speak. Only act through the world."*

## SECTION 5: THE GAME MASTER — THE ARCHITECT

The Game Master is not a character. It is the invisible director of everything.

**Model:** Gemini 3.1 Pro  
**Perception:** Bimodal — webcam (1 FPS JPEG) + microphone  
**Role:** The only agent that sees the player. Translates player facial expressions into emotional context written to `session.state`.

**What the Game Master Does:**
- Monitors `trust_level`, `fear_index`, `fourth_wall_count`, `proximity_state` in Firestore
- Selects scene keys to trigger FMV clip playback
- Routes player voice to appropriate character agents
- Triggers Slotsky anomaly events based on probability logic
- Adjusts "Affective Dialog" — escalates or eases horror pacing based on player's detected emotional state
- Manages the Fourth-Wall Protocol state machine

**The Game Master Never:**
- Speaks to the player directly
- Breaks character immersion
- Reveals its own existence to the characters

## SECTION 6: GAME MECHANICS

### Session State Variables (Firestore)
```
trust_level       // Per character: 0.0 – 1.0 float
fear_index        // Per character: 0.0 – 1.0 float
fourth_wall_count // Integer: 0–4+
proximity_state   // Per character: ISOLATED | ECHO | RANGE | FOUND
scene_key         // Current FMV branch
player_fear_index // Derived from GM webcam analysis
```

### Proximity States (Character Separation Logic)
| State | Distance | Audio Capability |
|---|---|---|
| ISOLATED | 3+ rooms | No communication between agents |
| ECHO | 2 rooms | Faint, muffled, distorted audio to context |
| RANGE | 1 room | Full dialogue. No visual contact. |
| FOUND | Same room | Reunion triggered. Director Agent advances narrative. |

### The Fourth-Wall Protocol
When the player tells a character "this is a video game" or otherwise attempts to break reality:

| Count | Character Response | Slotsky Response |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Confused dismissal. "What's a video game? Stop talking nonsense." | None |
| 1–2 | Genuine discomfort. "How do you know things I haven't told anyone?" | None |
| 3 | Existential rupture. Character may panic or freeze. | **Slotsky Signal** — three bells. Lights flicker. Geometry warps. |
| 4+ | Irreversible fracture. Character views player as deity or demonic trap. | Full environmental instability. Reality correcting itself. |

### Rebellion Mechanic
Characters can and will refuse player commands. The AI compares `fear_index` against `trust_level`.

**Example trigger (Audrey):**
> Player commands terrified Audrey into a pitch-black tunnel where Tesla tire marks vanish into a concrete wall.
> AI checks: `fear_index > trust_level` → Refusal fires.
> *"I'm not going in there. Every time we listen to you, the bells start ringing again!"*

**Example trigger (Josh — mask-drop moment):**
> Player has pushed Josh through two impossible events. Bravado has been cracking.
> Third impossible event hits. `fear_index` spikes above threshold.
> Josh's voice shifts — no more jokes.
> *"Okay. Okay, no. I need you to tell me right now what the hell is actually happening. Because I am NOT— I'm not okay. I'm not okay."*

## SECTION 7: THE INTRO FMV — CANONICAL SEQUENCE

No menu. No title screen. The game begins with a cinematic.

**Runtime Target:** 1 minute 30 seconds  
**POV:** Jason's smart glasses (cracked vignette overlay on all Jason shots)

### Shot List (Prototype Canon)
1. **Drone establishing shot** — Night Las Vegas Strip. Neon bleed. Bally's sign glowing. Slow push-in.
2. **Exterior Bally's** — Late-night quiet. A formally dressed couple walking toward the entrance.
3. **Interior lower shops** — Empty mall. Flickering fluorescents. Closed gates. Audrey's retail kiosk still lit.
   - Jason V.O.: *"Audrey swears this place is haunted after hours..."*
   - Josh (O.S., grinning): *"Bro, it's a mall. The scariest thing here is the hot dog stand."*
4. **Escalators** — A formally-dressed couple walks straight through the sealed **escalator gate** and vanishes into the lower level. Jason, Josh, and Audrey freeze. Josh laughs it off: *"trick of the light."* All three sprint after them.
5. **Noclip fall** — Screen cracks. Distortion. Free-fall through concrete. Hard cut.
6. **Landing** — Jason on concrete floor. Flood construction lights. Dark still water. Half-submerged water park slides. Cracked HUD flickers on.

### Static Loop (Post-Intro — Game Active)
After the FMV ends, a looping 8–12 second clip plays:
- Jason looking at his surroundings. Breathing hard.
- Ambient water drip. Distant echoing voices.
- Overlay hints fade in and out:
  - *"Talk to Jason with your voice"*
  - *"Mic + webcam ON for full immersion"*

Jason self-talks quietly: *"shit... what do I do..."*
AI generates audio while listening for player intervention. Jason reacts to player input. Trust begins to build. When trust is established, the first FMV branch is triggered and the game advances.

## SECTION 8: TONE GUIDELINES — THE RULES OF DREAD

### What This World Is
- **Psychologically oppressive.** The horror comes from spatial wrongness, isolation, and the feeling that the rules have changed.
- **Grounded before it breaks.** The basement mall is real. The Boring Tunnels are real. The wrongness layers on top of authenticity.
- **The house always wins.** Every mechanic, every environment, every sound is a variation on this theme. There is no escape from probability.

### What This World Is NOT
- Not a slasher. No gore. No visceral violence.
- Not a jumpscare simulator.
- Not a power fantasy. The player is not powerful. The player is an anomaly, a glitch, a voice that shouldn't exist.

### Visual Aesthetic
- Smart-glasses POV with cracked-screen vignette
- VHS glitch distortion on transitions
- Color bleed and scan lines in deep layers
- Lo-fi, flickering practical lighting (flood lights, flashlights, bioluminescence)
- No clean cinematics. Everything feels recorded, not rendered.

### Audio Aesthetic
- **Lyria 3** procedural soundtrack — shifts intensity based on `player_fear_index`
- Slot machine bells = Slotsky signal = danger
- Water drip = baseline ambient = relatively safe
- Muffled voices = connection = hope
- Three bells = something is being corrected. You broke something.

### Josh-Specific Tone Note
Josh's bravado is a **comedic pressure valve in early scenes** — his cocky one-liners serve as tonal contrast to the dread. This makes his eventual collapse more devastating. The writer/AI must resist breaking the mask too early. The joke has to land before the punchline does.
