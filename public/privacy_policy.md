# Privacy Policy — Liminal Sin

**Effective Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Project Owner:** J.W. / Mycelia Interactive

## 1) Plain-English Summary

Liminal Sin uses microphone and camera input to power live, real-time gameplay features.

- We do **not** intentionally store your camera feed or microphone audio.
- We do **not** sell, rent, trade, or broker your personal data.
- We do **not** use your personal data to train AI models owned by us.
- We do **not** run ads based on your camera/audio activity.
- We design the system to minimize retention and avoid unnecessary collection.

Because core functionality depends on third-party infrastructure (including Google/Gemini and related cloud services), data may be processed by those services during live interactions. Their handling is governed by their own terms and privacy policies.

## 2) Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to Liminal Sin websites, demos, and related services operated by Mycelia Interactive that reference this policy.

## 3) What We Process

To deliver live multimodal gameplay, the application may process:

- **Audio input** from your microphone (live interaction).
- **Video input** from your camera (live interaction and emotional-state gameplay logic).
- **Technical metadata** (for example, basic logs such as timestamps, request health, and error status).

## 4) Camera and Audio Data Handling

Our intent and implementation design are:

1. Camera and audio inputs are handled as **ephemeral, live-session streams**.
2. We do **not** intentionally persist raw camera/video frames or raw microphone recordings in our systems.
3. We do **not** create user voice/profile databases from this input.
4. We do **not** intentionally keep replay archives of user camera/audio.

If a technical fault, provider behavior, or legal requirement causes temporary processing outside our control, that processing is governed by the relevant third-party provider’s terms.

## 5) No Sale / No Data Brokerage

Mycelia Interactive does not sell, rent, trade, or broker personal data collected through Liminal Sin.

## 6) No AI Training by Us Using Personal Data

Mycelia Interactive does not use your personal camera/audio data to train our own AI systems.

Where third-party AI/cloud providers are used, their platform-level processing and model policies apply under their own contracts and privacy terms.

## 7) Third-Party Services and Responsibility Boundaries

Liminal Sin may rely on third-party infrastructure and APIs (for example, Google Cloud and Gemini services) to function.

- These providers may process data in transit to deliver requested features.
- Mycelia Interactive does not control every internal practice of those providers.
- **J.W. and Mycelia Interactive are not responsible for independent data practices of Google, Gemini, or their affiliates.**

Users should review third-party privacy policies and terms directly before use.

## 8) Data Minimization Commitment

We aim to configure systems to:

- collect the least data needed to run the experience,
- avoid long-term retention,
- avoid unnecessary sharing,
- and reduce personal-data exposure to major entities wherever technically feasible.

## 9) Security Measures

We use reasonable technical and organizational safeguards designed to protect data in transit and service integrity. No internet-based system can guarantee absolute security.

## 10) Children’s Privacy

Liminal Sin is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). If you believe a child has submitted personal data, contact us for review and deletion.

## 11) Your Choices and Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, or limitation of personal data we control.

To make a request, contact the project owner at the contact channel published in the repository/project site.

## 12) Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect product, legal, or infrastructure changes. Updates will be posted with a revised Effective Date.

## 13) Legal Disclaimer

This policy describes operational intent and current implementation goals. It is not legal advice and does not override binding third-party platform terms, legal obligations, or compulsory lawful requests.
